# Author's Notes

&gt; Personal reflections by lordekz ([@Khamit](https://github.com/Khamit)) on the origin and development of this theory.

---

## How This Began

I did not set out to create a theory. I had a thought — a single image, really — of something that exists but cannot be explained. Not because it is hidden. Not because it is complex. But because explanation itself breaks down when it touches it.

I wrote down the thought. Then I wrote down what follows from it. Then I noticed that what follows looks like a system. Not a complete system. A beginning.

## What I Am Not

I am not a philosopher. I am not a physicist. I am not a mathematician. I am someone who thinks about structures and notices patterns.

This means:
- I may use terms imprecisely
- I may reinvent ideas that already exist
- I may make errors that experts would spot immediately

I welcome all of these being pointed out. The goal is not to be original. The goal is to be **right** — or at least **interesting enough to be wrong in a productive way**.

## What I Hope For

I hope this theory provokes one of three responses:

1. **"This is wrong, and here is why"** — the best possible outcome. A refutation would clarify the boundaries.
2. **"This resembles X, which you should read"** — also excellent. I want to know what already exists.
3. **"This could be extended in direction Y"** — the ideal outcome. A living theory that grows.

## What I Fear

I fear this being dismissed as:
- Mysticism (it is not; it makes structural claims)
- Nihilism (it is not; it affirms the reality of the object)
- Solipsism (it is not; the object is real, independent, and external)

If you think it is any of these, please explain why. I may need to clarify the theory.

## On the Name

"Impossible Object" is a working title. It refers to:
- Impossible objects in art (Escher, Penrose triangle) — things that look coherent locally but are globally inconsistent
- The object's property of being impossible to fully describe
- The tension between "is" and "impossible" — the object exists, yet its existence defies categories

Better names may emerge.

## On the Future

I do not know if this theory will develop, wither, or be absorbed into something larger. I do know that the thought from which it came felt **true** in a way that few thoughts do. Not true as in "proven." True as in "this is a door I have not seen before."

I opened it. Now I am looking inside.
