## What is this?

This repository contains a theoretical framework proposing that **the fundamental entity is not an object, but a principle of absolute indescribability**.

We do not claim that a "thing" exists in the ordinary sense. We claim that there exists a **unified structural reason** why no finite system can construct a complete description of reality — including itself. What finite observers call an "impossible object" is simply the name they give to the source of all descriptive boundaries.

The framework is built on **six axioms plus a research program** that describe the consequences of this principle, rather than the principle itself. It argues that the boundaries of knowledge — incompleteness, uncomputability, quantum uncertainty, finite speed of light — are not separate limitations, but **different projections of a single informational horizon**.

---

## Core Claim

> **The fundamental entity is not the object, but the principle of absolute indescribability.** The "impossible object" is merely the name finite observers give to the source of all descriptive boundaries. The object and the informational horizon are **two aspects of one structure** — like curvature and the surface of a sphere. The sphere does not "create" curvature; curvature is the way the sphere exists.

This is not mysticism. It is a structural claim that shifts focus from "what exists" to **"why complete description is impossible"** — and what follows from that impossibility.

## The Six Axioms

| # | Axiom | Key Idea |
|---|-------|----------|
| 1 | **Information Horizon** | Complete knowledge = possession. Description and object are identical. |
| 2 | **Description Is Fundamental** | Not Shannon information, but Kolmogorov complexity (minimal description length) is the physical quantity. The object has no finite minimal description. |
| 3 | **Understanding and Object Are Dual** | Not causality, but duality: the object and the horizon are two aspects of one structure. |
| 4 | **Only the Shadow Exists** | We interact only with projections, never the structure itself. Physical laws may be cross-sections. |
| 5 | **Control Is Impossible** | One cannot control the structure, only change one's position relative to it. |
| 6 | **Unified Principle of Limits** | All fundamental limits (Gödel, Turing, Heisenberg, c) are manifestations of one horizon. |
| 7 | **Principle of Description Preservation** | Nature does not preserve energy first. It preserves the **structure of description**. Energy is a special case. |

## Research Program

> **Do not look for the object. Look for what is impossible.**

The framework's central methodological directive: the principle never manifests positively, but always negatively — through what is principally inaccessible to any finite description. The limits of mathematics, logic, and computation are not defects of these disciplines — they are the **edges of the structure**.

See [`THEORY/07-research-program.md`](THEORY/07-research-program.md) for the full research program, including:
- Why "impossibilities" are the right target
- Time as the order in which a finite observer is forced to receive information
- Space as the geometry of description complexity (Kolmogorov distance)
- Light as the maximum rate of description change, not motion
- The universe as lazy evaluation: it computes only what is necessary to maintain consistency among local observers
- The search for invariants across all possible universes

## Why this matters

Most theories of "the unknowable" fall into one of three traps:

1. **Theological** — they invoke a deity, which makes the claim unfalsifiable and culturally bound.
2. **Mathematical** — they treat incompleteness as a property of formal systems, not of reality.
3. **Physical** — they seek a "theory of everything," assuming the universe is fully describable.

This framework avoids all three. It does not claim to describe the source. It claims to describe the **structural consequences of indescribability** on any finite information system — including human minds, computers, and physical measurement apparatus.

The shift from "object" to "principle" makes the theory more robust: we no longer need to defend the existence of a mysterious entity. We only need to show that a unified structural explanation of known limits is possible — and fruitful.

This is an **early-stage theoretical framework**, not a peer-reviewed scientific theory. It is offered as a provocation — a way of looking at the boundaries of knowledge that may or may not prove fruitful.
